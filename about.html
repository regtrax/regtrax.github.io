About

The global deployment of blockchain technologies could dramatically change the way the worldâ€™s financial systems work. Yet despite this potential sea change, most governments have been slow to engage in a meaningful way within their respective regulatory regimes. This collectively cautious approach of policymakers has created a broad sense of uncertainty amongst companies in the blockchain space, and given the lack of any comprehensive or integrated regulatory framework, there is little reason to believe this state of uncertainty will change anytime soon.
In light of this, the Stanford CodeX Blockchain Group created the RegTrax Forum with the goal of facilitating critical conversations amongst and between policymakers, technologists and academics. Such conversations can play an important role in helping thaw the stalemate characterizing the current relationship between government regulators and blockchain technology builders. The RegTrax Forum seeks to open up avenues for communication and learning through its curated forum designed to encourage conversation amongst participants in a safe space, while giving thought leaders an intellectual sandbox to thoughtfully develop regulatory approaches and frameworks.


The Stanford CodeX Blockchain Group RegTrax Forum seeks to:

  • Host open, wide-ranging discussions on key issues in the space
  • Connect regulators, technologists, and academics from around the world
  • Create a repository of information pertaining to emerging regulatory regimes
  • Encourage public participation and offer guidance to these discussions
  • Disseminate relevant data in a clear, safe and unbiased manner

Mission

The RegTrax Forum seeks to provide a space for regulators, policymakers, technology stakeholders and academics to engage in open, thoughtful discussions about the current and future state of blockchain regulation. The goal is to meaningfully contribute to the development of effective regulatory approaches that encourage innovation while protecting consumers and investors.

Rules

RegTrax follows a set of prescribed rules to ensure open, transparent discussion.

  1. Chatham House Rule: No speaker attribution unless explicitly granted
  2. Civility: Keep conversations civil - no discriminatory speech of any kind will be tolerated
  3. Fair use: We discourage project endorsments, although fair use in mentioning a project is acceptable

Format

RegTrax has a format designed to keep an engaging pace, while also allowing sufficient time to consider these difficult issues. Our goal is to strike a balance between the competing concerns of allowing ample opportunities for the general public to engage and shape the discussion, while also carving out a safe space for thought leaders in the industry to exchange their views off the record. These "closed" discussions are designed to encourage frank or even controversial speech, without putting anyone in a position where they may be risking their career (or worse) as they struggle to navigate these difficult issues. We believe that the insights gleaned from these conversations belong to the public, and we are committed to releasing a faithful transcript of these conversations—regardless of content—after we've de-identified the statements.
To that end, our format is as follows:

  • Each cycle last 3 weeks, with one cycle per month
  • Weeklong public discussion and comment periods, in which anyone can comment, ask questions, and otherwise engage with our topic
  • Timed release of de-identified "closed" conversations to be released within a week of each cycle ending
