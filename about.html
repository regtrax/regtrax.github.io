About

Blockchain may very well represent a new frontier in technological development. Despite this (or, perhaps, in recognition of this), most governments have been fairly slow in putting official regulations in place. The functional result of this caution is an uncertain landscape and a widespread lack of any kind of comprehensive or integrated legal framework. In light of this, RegTrax was formed as a new initiative from the Stanford CodeX Blockchain Group with the goal of facilitating these critical conversations. In a lot of ways, governments are at a stalemate with industry. Regulators have seen enough to chill free and open development, but not quite enough to feel comfortable setting down permanent legislation. Further complicating issues is the tremendous and volatile cryptocurrency market creating an environment where any signals—whether intentional or not—are immediately taken as gospel and spread through social media. Academics and policymakers can't have the frank discussions that are necessary to begin sketching out regulations. And this has a trickledown effect on industry and the public, who can feel as if they're not getting the information they need to make informed decisions. RegTrax aims to change this with a carefully crafted forum designed to encourage conversation, protect participants, and manage expectations, while giving thought leaders the sandbox they so desperately need.


Broadly speaking, we strive to:

  • Host transparent, theoretical discussions
  • Connect academics and regulators from around the world
  • Create an evolving repository of this information
  • Invite the public to participate and guide the direction
  • Disseminate the data in a safe and unbiased manner

Mission

RegTrax has a simple mission: to provide a forum for regulators, academics, policymakers, and industry players to engage in free discussions about the current and future state of blockchain regulations.
There are a number of factors that make these conversations difficult. First and foremost is the reality that blockchain technology is very much intertwined with cryptocurrencies, and many of our discussants realize that their words can have enormous economic repercussions. And yet, policy can't move forward without honesty and collaboration. Our platform is designed to house this dialogue in such a way that contributors can feel safe expressing their views, but not worry about their words having undue impact or, in the case of someone advocating for an unpoular position, adversely affecting their careers. We hope to faciliate both the communication around and unbiased release of this crucial information.

Rules

RegTrax follows a set of prescribed rules to ensure open, transparent discussion.

  1. Chatham House Rule
  2. Vetted Discussants
  3. Weekly Discussion Cycles

Format

RegTrax has a format designed to keep an engaging pace, while also allowing ample time to consider these difficult issues.

  • 3-week cycles
  • Weeklong public discussion periods
  • Timed release of discussant data
