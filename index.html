Our Mission

We seek to hold open & unbiased discussions about regulatory & policy developments in the blockchain space

Regulators + Adademics + Technologists

We believe that one of the major issues facing blockchain technology today is the lack of understanding between key stakeholders. To this end, RegTrax hosts a neutral, unbiased forum where policymakers, builders, researchers, and the public can come together to discuss and learn.
Each RegTrax cycle is 3 weeks, with the first week set aside for discussion and research, the second for public discussion and comment, and the third for a "closed" discussion among invited thought leaders, where they can share controversial opinions and explore incomplete theories. After each "closed" session concludes, RegTrax will de-identify the participants and information, and release a faithful transcript to the public.

About RegTrax

RegTrax is an open discussion forum for lawyers, academics, and technologists to discuss the most pressing regulatory issues facing the blockchain space. We operate under the Chatham House Rule, and are a part of the Stanford CodeX Blockchain Group.
